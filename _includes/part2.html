How does respected media compare to popular media?



Who gives a voice to females?

In order to assess the difference between respected and popular media, a subset of quotes is analysed and quotes originating only from a shortlist of media sources are kept. These media were selected according to the following sources: respected and popular.

And how do they evolve over the years?

These two groups of media have a different reputation. While one could expect that "respected" media would perform better in terms of equal gender representation, this figure shows that it is not the case at all. In fact, a gap is present between popular and respected media every single year.

The increase in proportion of females over the years in either group is encouring (the drop in 2020 is likely to the reduced number of quotes collected for that year) and surely reflects the awareness on gender issues that has increasingly been voices, and mediatised to some extent

But what do females talk about?

When thinking of the possible differences between "respected" and "popular" media, the differences in topics or categories covered come to mind. Indeed, the type of news presented by the sources differ thematically, politically and in terms of audience. The overall proportion of females does not give insight into what they are quoted for and how this differs between the respected and popular media.

By analysing keywords used in the URL of the article or page containing the quote, the quotes themselves are sorted into different categories of news. This allows to see if there are trends between genders based on topics, and understand how this could explain the results observed for the proportion of feamles depending on the mdeia source.

The repartition of categories among the genders follows stereotypes: very few females are quoted in sports, while entertainment and culture present higher shares of female quotes, with more than 50% achieved in 2018 for popular entertainment.
